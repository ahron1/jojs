We, the builders of the app, see ourselves as users first. As users, we have always been fanatical about online privacy. For example, we do not even ask for your full name or complete date of birth, nor do we have fields to even mention this information. Privacy is baked in to the very fabric of the app. And Yes, the app is GDPR compliant by design. We were fortunate in that the GDPR was released just as were designing the app, so we were happy to build the recommendations right into the design of the app.

We could share with you a long lawyer-written document about our strict privacy policy, but instead we will summarize everything that matters into one paragraph. At the time of signing up, you are asked to enter your first name and email address and on your profile page, you can enter your birth year if you want. These are the only personal data we store about you. We also have a record of how you found the app (via google, another app or website, etc.). Given the functionality of the app, we also store the following information about your activity on the app - the photos that you were shown, and how you voted on each photo, the photos you uploaded, any messages you sent to us via the app, and how often you logged in and out. The only external script on the webapp is from Google analytics - which is a necessity, in order for us to see how many people are using the service and how. But that's it, really. Lastly, we would like to note that we do not place trackers on other websites to follow you around the internet.

The web app does not have sharing buttons because all such buttons are known to track users and place cookies on user devices - we consider this a breach of privacy. So we encourage you to simply use the share feature on your web browser and share us via your favorite communication or social media app. This small extra step for us is a giant leap towards privacy.
